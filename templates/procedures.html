    Lumbar Puncture

    A meticulous stepwise approach to cerebrospinal fluid collection via lumbar puncture.

    This procedure is commonly used to diagnose or rule out serious infections such as meningitis, or to relieve pressure from excess cerebrospinal fluid.

    1. Obtain informed consent after detailed explanation of indications, risks, and benefits.
    2. Position patient in lateral decubitus with knees flexed to maximize intervertebral space.
    3. Identify anatomical landmarks to locate L3-L4 or L4-L5 interspace for needle insertion.
    4. Perform full sterile preparation and administer local anesthesia meticulously.
    5. Advance spinal needle slowly into subarachnoid space to collect cerebrospinal fluid.
    6. Withdraw needle carefully, apply sterile dressing, and label samples accurately.
    7. Monitor patient for potential complications such as headache, bleeding, or neurological changes.
